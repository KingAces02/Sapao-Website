About Barangay Sapao

Barangay Sapao is a coastal community in the municipality of Guiuan, Eastern Samar, Philippines. It played a significant role during World War II as part of the expansive U.S. Naval Base in Guiuan, which served as a strategic point for Allied Forces in the Pacific. The barangay is home to the Parish of the Holy Cross, a Roman Catholic parish established as a quasi-parish in 1999 and formally canonically erected in 2004. The parish celebrates its feast day every May 7 in honor of the Holy Cross. Sapao is also known for its natural attractions, including Sapao Beach, with its white sands and diverse shells offering scenic views of the Pacific Ocean. The barangay hosts essential community facilities such as the Barangay Hall, Day Care Center, and Basketball Court. Additionally, Sapao is the location of a PAGASA weather station equipped with a Doppler radar system, contributing to regional weather monitoring and forecasting.

Community Development

Barangay Sapao continues to thrive through the dedication and hard work of its local officials. According to the one barangay official, their responsibilities are wide-ranging, focusing on maintaining peace, implementing community development projects, and improving the quality of life for their residents. Their duties include enforcing rules and regulations and ensuring that the community adheres to policies designed to promote order and growth. One of the projects currently being implemented is the “I Love You Sapao” initiative, which aims to foster unity and pride within the barangay. However, the project is still ongoing due to the officials' busy schedules, as they are currently preparing for the much-anticipated annual fiesta. Fishing remains the primary source of livelihood for many residents, given the barangay's proximity to the sea. Understanding the needs of their constituents, the barangay officials actively support working individuals and low-income families. They do this by facilitating programs from the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), and by initiating projects that benefit the most vulnerable members of their community. The fiesta of Sta. Cruz is a major event in Barangay Sapao, celebrated with great enthusiasm. Festivities include a vesper night and a solemn mass held at the local church, often officiated by a bishop. These events not only reflect the community’s devotion but also strengthen their cultural heritage and social ties. Barangay Sapao is home to two sitios: San Roque and Dumrog. Each sitio has its own unique celebrations and traditions, adding to the rich and diverse culture of the barangay. Through the collective efforts of its officials and residents, Barangay Sapao stands as a testament to the strength of community and the power of local governance.

About The Team

The 2nd year students of Eastern Samar State University of Guiuan aimed to help the Barangay of Sapao grow its tourism by making a website about the history and tourist spots in the said location. This project supports a shared goal of using technology to strengthen communities, promote tourism, and build local pride in the digital age.
